Our brand principles are the compass for every design, copy, and product decision. They descend from **Kind Utility**: utility-first at the core, human-first at the surface.

## 01 — Utility by Default

Everything we build must be useful first. Aesthetics, delight, and brand expression are layered on top of a foundation of genuine utility. If it doesn't help a teacher do their job better, it doesn't ship.

| Prioritise | Deprioritise |
| --- | --- |
| Core functionality that saves teachers time | Decorative elements that don't serve function |
| Clear information hierarchy | Complex animations that slow perceived speed |
| Accessible, universal design patterns | Novel interactions that require learning |
| Performance and reliability | Brand expression that compromises usability |

## 02 — Kind at Surface

The surface layer, what teachers see and touch first, must always feel warm and respectful: friendly copy, inviting colours, approachable type, interfaces that feel made by someone who cares.

| Prioritise | Deprioritise |
| --- | --- |
| Warm, encouraging language | Technical error codes or stack traces |
| Gentle error states with clear next steps | Cold, transactional language |
| Celebratory success moments | Dismissive or generic error messages |
| Approachable visual design | Intimidating or complex UI chrome |

## 03 — Calm Guidance

Guide without overwhelming. Like a calm, knowledgeable colleague, surface the right information at the right time. Progressive disclosure is the default: show less, reveal more on demand.

| Prioritise | Deprioritise |
| --- | --- |
| Progressive disclosure and contextual help | Showing all options at once |
| Sensible defaults that reduce decisions | Modal interruptions for non-critical info |
| Quiet, non-intrusive feedback | Dense, information-heavy layouts |
| White space and visual breathing room | Aggressive notifications or badges |

## 04 — Light Weight

Both in feel and in load. Fast and unburdened in perceived performance, visual density, cognitive load, and emotional weight. Every added element must justify its existence.

| Prioritise | Deprioritise |
| --- | --- |
| Fast load times and snappy interactions | Heavy imagery or background media |
| Minimal visual clutter | Multi-step processes when one step would do |
| Simple, focused task flows | Dense dashboards with competing elements |
| Light palettes and generous white space | Dark, heavy themes as default |

> **The one test:** does this help teachers work faster with less stress? If not, we don't build it.
