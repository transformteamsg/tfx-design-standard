## Anatomy

The master canvas is **640 × 640**. Every number below lives in that space; scale proportionally everywhere else.

- **Container** — a full-bleed rounded square, corner radius **240** (37.5% of the side). It is identical on all three icons. Reuse it; never redraw it.
- **Background** — solid **#0064FF**, edge to edge. No gradients, no transparency, no per-product colour — product accents live in the UI, the icon family stays blue.
- **Mark** — one white lowercase script form, normally the product's first letter (`tw`, `s`, `g`). One mark, one focal point. No wordmarks, no photos, no shadows or gloss.

## The construction grid

Every curve in the family sits on a circle — the marks are drawn from circle arcs, not freehand strokes. That shared geometry is what makes three different letters read as one family.

Placement rules:

- Keep the mark at least **78 from every edge** (12% of the canvas). Glow's ascender and descender sit exactly on this line — that is the limit, not the target.
- Let the mark fill **between half and three-quarters of the canvas** in its larger dimension. Smaller floats in the square; larger crowds it.
- Centre **optically, not mathematically**. CaseSync's visual centre sits about 4 units off true centre because the drawn mark demanded it — trust your eye, then verify at small sizes.
- Stroked marks use a stroke between **46 and 53** (about 7–8% of the side). CaseSync is 46; Glow is 53 with a 46 secondary stroke. Filled marks like `tw` keep their apparent stroke weight in the same band.

## Designing a new icon

1. Take the product name's first letter, lowercase, in the family's script style.
2. Draw every curve as a circle arc. If a curve doesn't sit cleanly on a circle, it isn't finished.
3. Place the mark on the grid: clear zone, size band, stroke weight.
4. Run it through the **Icon Generator** — the construction grid it infers from your drawing should verify cleanly. Wobbly curves show up as unverified circles; fix the drawing, not the report.
5. Test at 48 px and 24 px, on light and dark surfaces, in a row with the existing three. If you can't tell whose icon it is, simplify.

## Export

- The master is SVG at 640 × 640 with the rounded container baked in — use it on web and in-product surfaces.
- Raster exports: PNG at 1024, 512, 256, 128, 64, 48 and 32, in sRGB.
- App-store and launcher slots: export a **square, full-bleed version without rounded corners** — platforms apply their own mask, and a pre-rounded submission shows dark seams in the corners.

**Do:** one letter, one focal point · every curve a circle arc · solid blue and white only · optical centring · test tiny before shipping.

**Don't:** wordmarks or taglines inside the icon · gradients, shadows, gloss · redrawing or restyling the container · per-product background colours · marks crossing the clear zone.
