Our **voice** is constant; our **tone** adapts to context. This page presents three catalog controls for human readers — [CNT-3](/standards/catalog/cnt-3) (voice mechanics), [CNT-1](/standards/catalog/cnt-1) (error anatomy), and [SLP-9](/standards/catalog/slp-9) (AI-writing tells). The controls are normative; this page and the `tfx-content-style` skill both apply them, so the copy arrives already on-voice. Nobody, least of all non-native English speakers, should carry the UX-writing burden personally.

## Voice attributes

| We are | We are not |
| --- | --- |
| Warm but not sappy | Corporate-speak or jargon-heavy |
| Clear but not cold | Overly casual or slang-filled |
| Helpful but not pushy | Condescending or preachy |
| Professional but not stiff | Vague or wishy-washy |
| Confident but not arrogant | Salesy or hype-driven |

## Tone by context

| Context | Tone | Direction |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Success | Celebratory, brief | Acknowledge, don't gush |
| Error | Calm, helpful | What happened → what it means → what to do next |
| Onboarding | Encouraging | Lower the stakes; show the quick win |
| Destructive action | Sober, precise | Plain consequences, no drama ([CMP-2](/standards/catalog/cmp-2)) |
| Empty state | Inviting | Lead with the next action |
| Permission / data request | Transparent, plain | Say what's collected, why, and how it's used — before asking |

## Writing rules

[CNT-3](/standards/catalog/cnt-3) in practice — the voice mechanics applied to everyday copy.

- Second person ("you"), active voice, sentence case.
- Lead with the benefit, not the feature. One idea per sentence.
- Error messages guide the exit: never just state what broke; say how to fix it (CNT-1).
- Numerals for counts ("8 classes", not "eight"). Numbers and units separated by a space.
- Loading and follow-up actions end with an ellipsis character (…): "Saving…", "Rename…".
- No AI-writing tells: SLP-9 in the [control catalog](/standards/catalog) carries the full list, from buzzwords and em-dash chains to structural tells.
- Read it aloud. If it sounds robotic, rewrite it.
