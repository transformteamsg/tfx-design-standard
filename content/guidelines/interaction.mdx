---
title: Interaction
description: Progressive disclosure by default; forgiveness by design.
status: settled
---

- **Progressive disclosure is the default pattern.** Show the core path; reveal complexity on demand.
- **Prefer inline validation over modal errors.** Catch problems where they happen; on submit, focus the first error.
- **Lead with the teacher's next action** — in empty states, errors, and onboarding. No dead ends: every screen offers a next step or recovery path.
- **Sensible defaults over decisions.** Every choice you remove is a gift of time.
- **Forgiveness by design.** Undo over confirm where reversible; confirm with plain consequences where not (CMP-2). Warn before navigation when unsaved data could be lost.
- **Quiet feedback.** Non-intrusive confirmation; reserve interruptions for genuinely blocking moments. Complex multi-section tasks get a page, not a modal (SLP-10).
- **All states designed.** Empty, sparse, dense, loading, and error states for every view (CMP-3). Skeletons mirror final content to avoid layout shift.
- **Respect the seasonal rhythm.** Teachers' workflows spike at term start, exams, and reporting. Design for the stressed week, not the average one.
