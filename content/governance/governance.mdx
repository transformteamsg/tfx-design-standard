---
title: How this evolves
description: A living standard — owned like code, fed by evidence.
status: proposed
---

- **Living document.** If something feels off or outdated, speak up — or open a PR. Anyone in TransformX can propose a control, guideline, or tool. The bar for L0/L1 is high; the bar for L2 is evidence.
- **The ratchet.** The catalog grows only from observed failures, never speculation. Every escaped defect becomes a control proposal.
- **Decision records.** Significant decisions record options considered, the tradeoff named, waivers granted.
- **Waiver registry.** L1 waivers in one queryable place with approver and reason. Recurring waivers mean: fix the standard or fix the system.
- **Evidence base.** Teacher feedback as tagged, queryable evidence records; open pattern questions in one register with live status.
- **System time is not overhead.** A deliberate share of design capacity goes to improving the system — controls, checks, docs. An hour on a check script saves ten hours of review; only system work compounds.

## Measures

| Measure | What it tells us |
| --- | --- |
| First-pass check rate | Is the standard learnable? |
| Review cycle time | Are we actually faster? |
| Escaped defects | Is the catalog catching reality? |
| L1 waiver rate | Rising = fix the standard or fix the system |
| Debt register trend | Down and to the right |
| No-designer shipments | **The mission metric** |
| Evidence-closed OPQs | Are we closing debates by evidence or opinion? |
